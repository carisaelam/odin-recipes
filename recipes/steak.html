Steak

Description

A sizzling steak, perfectly seared to caramelized perfection, beckons with its tantalizing aroma. As the meat hits the grill, it releases a symphony of sizzles, promising a flavorful feast to come. Each bite offers a tender, juicy explosion of savory goodness, with a charred exterior giving way to a succulent interior. Whether cooked to a rare, medium, or well-done perfection, a steak captivates the senses with its rich umami flavor and hearty texture.

Accompanied by a medley of grilled vegetables or a simple salad, a steak embodies the epitome of indulgence and satisfaction. Its versatility allows for endless culinary creativity, whether seasoned simply with salt and pepper or adorned with a decadent sauce. With its primal allure and timeless appeal, a steak remains a beloved centerpiece of any hearty meal, inviting diners to savor each mouthwatering bite.

Ingredients

  • Steak (such as ribeye, sirloin, or filet mignon)
  • Salt
  • Black pepper
  • Garlic powder
  • Onion powder
  • Butter (for basting)
  • Fresh herbs (such as thyme or rosemary)
  • Olive oil (for searing)
  • Worcestershire sauce (optional)
  • Balsamic vinegar (optional)

Steps

  1. Remove the steak from the refrigerator and let it sit at room temperature for 30-60 minutes to ensure even cooking.
  2. Season both sides of the steak generously with salt, black pepper, garlic powder, and onion powder.
  3. Preheat a skillet or grill over high heat. Brush the steak with olive oil to prevent sticking.
  4. Once the skillet or grill is hot, place the steak on it and sear for 2-3 minutes on each side to achieve a golden crust.
  5. Lower the heat to medium and continue cooking the steak to the desired level of doneness, flipping it occasionally.
  6. Optional: Add a knob of butter and fresh herbs (such as thyme or rosemary) to the skillet or grill and baste the steak with the melted butter for extra flavor.
  7. Check the internal temperature of the steak using a meat thermometer. For rare, it should be around 120-125°F (49-52°C); for medium-rare, 130-135°F (54-57°C); for medium, 140-145°F (60-63°C); for medium-well, 150-155°F (66-68°C); for well-done, 160°F (71°C) and above.
  8. Once the steak reaches the desired doneness, remove it from the heat and let it rest for 5-10 minutes to allow the juices to redistribute.
  9. Optional: Drizzle the steak with Worcestershire sauce or balsamic vinegar for added flavor before serving.
  10. Slice the steak against the grain and serve hot, garnished with fresh herbs if desired. Enjoy!
